About Me

Research Computational Hydrologist

Dr. Mahesh Lal Maskey is a Research Computational Hydrologist with expertise in hydrologic modeling, hydroeconomic analysis, and climate-resilient water management. His interdisciplinary work integrates process-based and machine learning models to improve understanding of evapotranspiration, groundwater dynamics, and agricultural sustainability under changing climate and land-use conditions. He has held research positions at USDA-ARS, UC Davis, and UC Merced, contributing to stakeholder-driven modeling frameworks, high-performance computing platforms, and applied decision-support tools used in the Mississippi Delta, California, and internationally. His doctoral research provided new insights into rainfall and streamflow variability across scales by simulating and analyzing the geometric complexity of hydrologic records using deterministic fractal-multifractal methods. His outreach and extension efforts focus on translating science into actionable insights for farmers, policymakers, and water managers.
